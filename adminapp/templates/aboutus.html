Our mission is to create a digital environment where readers can trust the information they consume. Using advanced Fact Check Api, we detect and flag fake news, empowering users with accurate, fact-checked content.

Key Terminology

Understanding the language of misinformation detection

To engage with fake news detection, understanding key terms is essential. Below are definitions to navigate our platform and the broader conversation about misinformation:

Misinformation

False or inaccurate information spread without intent to deceive, often believed to be true by the sharer.

Disinformation

Deliberately false information created and spread to deceive, aiming to influence or mislead audiences.

Clickbait

Sensationalized content designed to attract clicks, often using misleading or exaggerated headlines.

Echo Chamber

An environment where individuals are exposed only to opinions aligning with their own, reinforcing biases.

Confirmation Bias

The tendency to favor information that confirms existing beliefs while ignoring contradictory evidence.

Deepfake

AI-generated synthetic media that convincingly alters a person’s likeness in audio or video content.

Source Credibility

A measure of a source’s trustworthiness based on accuracy, expertise, transparency, and journalistic standards.

Digital Literacy

The ability to critically evaluate, utilize, and create content using digital technologies and the internet.
